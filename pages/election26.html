Peter X. K. Song

Professor of Biostatistics
School of Public Health, University of Michigan

Professional Profile
Vision Statement

As Chair of this Interest Group, my primary focus will be the continued promotion of Stats Up AI, with the goal of guiding the Interest Group (IG) toward becoming a formal ASA section within the next few years. Central to this effort is articulating a clear scientific vision that emphasizes the development of principled statistical foundations for artificial intelligence, including interpretability, robustness, reproducibility, and responsible AI practices. I will lead the development of a strategic growth plan that defines both short-term objectives (three to five years) and long-term goals (five to ten years), providing a sustainable and transparent pathway for the IG’s growth and governance.

To strengthen the IG’s intellectual leadership and scholarly impact, I will promote high-quality programming, including organized and invited sessions at JSM, workshops, tutorials, and webinar series that highlight emerging research at the intersection of statistics and AI. Education and workforce development will be a core priority, with initiatives aimed at supporting students and early-career researchers through mentorship, career development activities, and guidance on statistics/AI curriculum development.

To attract and actively engage junior researchers, I will seek industrial support to establish research awards or other forms of recognition that promote innovative and impactful statistical contributions to AI or AI contributions to statistics. At the same time, I will work to broaden the IG’s membership by fostering meaningful collaboration with industry researchers and practitioners, ensuring that methodological advances are informed by real-world applications. I will also promote broad participation across career stages, institutional backgrounds, and communities.

Hongtu Zhu

Kenan Distinguished Professor of Biostatistics
The University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill

Professional Profile
Vision Statement

My vision for the future of Stats Up AI is to establish it as a leading, community-driven platform at the intersection of statistics, artificial intelligence, and domain science. Over the next few years, I plan to continue building high-quality biomedical datasets, scalable pipelines, and authoritative review papers that set methodological and applied standards for the field. In parallel, I will expand Stats Up AI’s educational and outreach mission by organizing short courses focused on the integration of AI and statistics, particularly emphasizing rigor, interpretability, and real-world impact. I also plan to grow our webinar series to foster regular dialogue among statisticians, AI researchers, and domain experts, creating a vibrant forum for sharing ideas, mentoring junior scholars, and shaping the future research agenda in trustworthy and scientifically grounded AI.

Candidate for Program Chair (2026)

Back to directory

Wenyi Wang

Professor
Department of Bioinformatics and Computational Biology and Department of Biostatistics
The University of Texas M. D. Anderson Cancer Center

Professional Profile
Vision Statement

Given my research portfolio at the intersection of statistical models and biomedical sciences, cancer genomics in particular, I am well positioned to advocate on what are the important open opportunities for statisticians and statistics in AI for biomedical sciences. I believe that over the next two years, growing our interest group membership, encouraging collaborations and disseminating the knowledge of existing opportunities will be crucial. We may achieve this by continuing our webinar series, building short courses, workshops, symposiums and networking events. Our redesigned website as well as the google interest group will serve as vital initial point for information dissemination. I will also build a larger workforce in collaboration with the outreach officer in order to support functions of our future programs. This team will also generate new ideas on programs and activities to support our goals. Finally, I will work with the former program chair and the executive committee to ensure other areas of statistics in AI are sufficiently addressed. Metrics and documentations will be developed to track the success of initiatives and programs. In conclusion, I aim to strengthen the impact of StatsUpAI and position it as a leader in advocating for the role of statistics in transforming biomedical research.

Candidate for Program Chair-Elect (2026)

Statement and profile information

Back to directory

Linjun Zhang

Associate Professor
Department of Statistics, Rutgers University

Professional Profile
Vision Statement

Stats Up AI can become the go-to community hub that helps statisticians not only keep up with AI, but shape it, by pairing methodological depth with practical impact. Over the next few years, I envision three high-value topics of activities: (1) signature talks that bring together statisticians, biostatisticians, and computer scientists. For example, webinar/seminar series, fireside conversations, and topical short courses; (2) high-quality shared resources that accelerate research and reproducibility, including curated domain datasets, up-to-date reviews, and ready-to-use AI tools that facilitate best practical data analysis; and (3) community building and professional development, such as mentoring networks and AI-assisted data analysis competition (we may also create some benchmark dataset ourselves). Together, these activities would position Stats Up AI as an ASA-aligned platform that empowers statisticians to lead at the interface of AI-enabled scientific discovery and statistical thinking.
