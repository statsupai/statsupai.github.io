Name & Scope

Official Name

The name of this organization is the “Stats Up Artificial Intelligence” Interest Group of the American Statistical Association (ASA). herein after referred to as the Interest Group.

Scope

The Interest Group promotes the integration of statistical methodologies and innovations in Artificial Intelligence (AI), and expands the role of statisticians in AI research, application, and leadership. The Interest Group's main focus will be empowering statisticians to better embrace emerging challenges and opportunities in the broad ecosystem of artificial intelligence research and applications.

Core efforts include organizing working groups, conferences, workshops, panels, short courses, and webinars on timely topics, highlighting research frontiers, data and workflows, and computational and curricular resources.

Objectives

  • Foster collaboration among statisticians to contribute significantly to AI research and applications
  • Modernize statistical education and curricula to equip future statisticians with skills and knowledge for the AI age
  • Pursue resources and develop shared infrastructures to support AI research within the statistical community
  • Create a robust network for mentoring and nurturing statisticians to lead and excel in AI initiatives

Membership

Membership is open to anyone interested in expanding the presence of statistics and statisticians in AI.

Who can join
Anyone interested
ASA membership
Not required for members (required for Chair & Chair-Elect)
Dues
No dues

Meetings

The Interest Group will hold regular meetings and workshops to facilitate collaboration, share knowledge, and plan activities. The meeting structure includes:

Annual Business Meeting

The annual business meeting of the Interest Group shall be held in connection with the annual Joint Statistical Meetings and shall be announced in advance, preferably in the program for that meeting. The Interest Group officers will present activities and progress of the Interest Group.

Quarterly Virtual Meetings

Held four times a year to share ongoing projects by working groups, new opportunities, and member research highlights.

Special Interest Meetings

Organized as needed to address specific topics or initiatives within the Interest Group.

Workshops, Short Courses, and Webinars

Organized as needed to provide educational opportunities and foster skill development in AI-related topics.

Business may also be conducted with the Interest Group members by email, website, and social media posts.

Working Groups

The Interest Group forms up to two working groups each year. Working groups are specialized teams focused on specific topics or projects aligned with the group’s goals.

  1. Proposal & discussion: Any member may propose working groups before or during the Annual Business Meeting.
  2. Selection: After the Annual Business Meeting, officers (in consultation with the Advisory Board) identify up to two working groups to establish each year.
  3. Chairs: Working group chairs are appointed by the Interest Group officers.
  4. Inclusion: Working groups are open to members and should foster an inclusive environment.
  5. Reporting: Working group chairs report progress at quarterly virtual meetings.

Advisory Board

An Advisory Board provides strategic guidance and expert advice to the Interest Group. The advisory board will consist of prominent leaders and scholars in the fields of statistics and AI. Members are selected by the Interest Group officers based on their expertise and contributions to the fields of statistics and AI.

Size
Up to five members
Term
Three years (reappointment possible)
Industry representation
At least one member from the private sector

Responsibilities

  • Advise on direction and activities of the Interest Group
  • Help identify resources, funding opportunities, and partnerships
  • Support educational and professional development programs
  • Serve as ambassadors within the broader scientific and professional community

The Advisory Board is invited to attend quarterly virtual meetings and the annual business meeting to review the Interest Group's progress, to provide feedback on initiatives, and to offer strategic recommendations.

Officers & Governance

Officer Positions

  • Chair
  • Chair-Elect
  • Program Chair
  • Program Chair-Elect
  • Outreach Chair
  • Secretary
Terms
One year for most officers; two years for Outreach Chair and Secretary/Treasurer
Annual transition
Chair-Elect and Program Chair-Elect automatically succeed on January 1

Role Summary

  • Chair: overall leadership and direction; organizes Annual Business Meeting and Quarterly Virtual Meetings; represents the Interest Group.
  • Chair-Elect: assists Chair; plans initiatives and working groups; steps in during Chair’s absence.
  • Program Chair / Program Chair-Elect: lead programming at conferences, workshops, webinars, and educational/networking events.
  • Outreach Chair: manages external communications, partnerships, and collaboration with other organizations/societies in AI.
  • Secretary/Treasurer: financial matters, meeting minutes, and membership communications.
Appointments
The Chair and Chair-Elect select and appoint the Outreach Chair and the Secretary.
Vacancies
If Chair or Program Chair becomes vacant, the Chair-Elect or Program Chair-Elect becomes Chair or Program Chair, respectively, with terms extending through the following year.

Nomination & Election

Nominating Committee

The Interest Group’s immediate past Chair shall serve as Chair of the Nominating Committee. The Interest Group’s immediate past Program Chair will also serve on this committee. The Chair will also nominate one person to serve on the Nominating Committee.

Timeline

  • By September 1: The Nominating Committee submits nominations to the Secretary and the Chair for each position to be filled.
  • Petition route: Any member nominated by petition of 5 or more members appears on the annual ballot, provided the petition is presented to the Secretary and Chair by September 1.
  • By November 1: Balloting shall be carried out by the Past Chair for electing Interest Group officers and completed by November 1.
  • Who receives ballots: Ballots shall be sent to all those who express an interest in the Interest Group through the Interest Group intake Google form.

Amendments

Amendments may be proposed by officers or by petition signed by at least 15 members, submitted to the officers.

Proposed amendments are submitted to members for vote at the time of the annual election of officers. If approved by a majority of members voting, the amendment is approved.

An approved amendment is effective immediately unless otherwise specified.
